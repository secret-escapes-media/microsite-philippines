A resplendent collection of over 7,000 island paradises, where clear shallow waters lap endless sand banks and hidden coves – a treat for explorers and deck-chair loungers alike.

Discover a culinary tradition steeped in Spanish and southeast Asian influences that is characterised by a lip-smacking medley of spices, cooked up by a people who are renowned for their warmth and generous hospitality. Tropical climes offering endless sun-kissed lounging add to the mix, leaving you with the ingredients for serene escapes in a veritable paradise.

Explore the limestone crags that silently pierce the waters of Bacuit Bay and paint an impressive scene from above or below the warm water. Immerse yourself in the colourful vivacity of the country’s fiestas, bedecked with technicolour dress and hypnotic dancing. Unpick the sprawling metropolis of Manila, or simply feel the crisp-white sand under your toes on White Beach in Boracay until the glowing sun dips and sinks below the luminous horizon.

sections: - title: An Island Sprawl class: island link: explore/palawan/ content: >

The Philippine islands are consistently voted the most cherished in the world and ogling a few pictures makes it easy to see why.

Crystal-clear waters reveal schools of tropical fish darting around beneath your feet, undeterred. Startlingly-white sands cushion wistful strolls at sunset, while Bancas dart from island to island and fishermen unload their hauls on makeshift piers. The island life will quickly enchant you, mesmerising any visitor at first glance.

- title: Curious Cooking class: cooking link: explore/visayas/#section-cebu content: >

The food of the Philippines is a sumptuous medley of fiery spices, tropical produce and a curated journey through heat, sourness, and delicacy.

Trump cards like balut and dinuguan, the rather stomach-turning fertilized duck egg and offal in blood dishes, don’t do Filipino cooking justice. With influence from Malaysia, China and most interestingly Spain (owing to a colonial past), you’ll find unique dishes like succulent lechon, classic adobo, rich kare kare, and all washed down with the Filipino fave, rum! Throw in endless snacking on dried mango and you have a wholesome, delicious recipe for a memorable stay.

- title: Dynamic Culture class: culture link: explore/luzon/ content: >

Pre-colonial heritage has intertwined seamlessly with a strong Spanish influence, offering a colourful menagerie of traditions and festivities.

With a remarkably 80% Catholic population, Spanish roots offer up colourful fiestas, festivals marking saints days throughout the year. Karaoke is also huge – a plethora of bars offer shrieks and pitch-perfect renditions much to the delight of, well, everyone. Blame the American occupation…

- title: Effortless Indulgence class: indulgence link: explore/visayas/#highlight-boracay content: >

Effortless relaxation awaits – from catatonic sunlounger bathing to groan-inducing beachfront massages, the Philippines is set to indulge you with every whim that ensures total comfort.

For those that seek more adventure, the islands offer superb diving, with WWII wrecks and acclaimed coral reefs to explore. Wildlife lovers can revel in this bountiful land, where new animals are still being discovered.
